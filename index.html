Overview


UP Entrepreneurs' Club (UPEC) is a socio-civic organization duly recognized by the Student Organization and Activities Division (SOAD) of the University of the Philippines Los Baños. Its founders, Gordian Jose S. Yap and Mark Francis Hamoy, shared one dream, which was to establish a an organization for young people that would promote entrepreneurship and the spirit of enterprise. Hence, the creation of UP Entrepreneurs' Club in 1990 and was later recognized in 1991. Through the years, the UP Entrepreneurs' Club has managed to sustain itself as a cohesive group of dedicated individuals motivated to achieve the vision and pursue the raison d' etre of the organization. Now, the UP Entrepreneur's Club continues its existence as a group of dynamic and highly motivated young people, meeting the challenges and responding to the opportunities that lie ahead. it shall remain, as it has always been, a home for that firebrand individual who is Young, Enterprising, and Truly Filipino.



Mission

Promote the spirit of entrepreneurship among members and in the community as well.

Vision

As young, enterprising and Filipino entrepreneurs, it is the members' duty to provide business opportunities, whether big or small in order to boost the economy and accordingly provide employment to fellow Filipinos.

Objectives

Unleash more economic opportunities through entrepreneurship brief description: a socio-civic organization that helps motivate individuals to uphold the spirit of entrepreneurship.

Brand Conscious

A product and brand-awareness quiz contest

Brand Conscious is UPEC's annual quiz contest, featuring trivias about local and international products and services. Last year, for our 22nd anniversary, we had over P22,000 worth of cash and gift ceritificates for the winners of Brand Conscious.

ABAKarunungan

Storytelling to kids in orphanages

The ABAKarunungan project was born during one of UPEC’s general assembly when the topic about conducting an outreach program was brought up. Being innovative is one of the unique characteristics of entrepreneurs, so instead of the usual outreach programs we opted for something more distinctive, something that will truly be ours.

The ABAKarunungan project strives to reach out to children, especially the less fortunate, and encourage them to appreciate reading story books in this era of modern technology and use it as a means of entertainment. Its aim is to help children realize that reading inspires imagination that fuels innovation in the future.

As this was our first time to execute our plans regarding the ABAKarunungan project, we didn’t expect that we would get support from various publishers and book distributors but the organization took the risk and surprisingly, five of the many publishers of whom our sponsorship letter were sent agreed to participate in the said project. Adarna House, Blink, Museo Pambata, Scholastic and Vibal Publishing House willingly donated books, which summed up to 150 plus books, for our chosen orphanage. Seeing that this event was a successful as well as fulfilling event, the org decided for it to be a permanent activity. This project serves as an additional activity for the UPEC, another way of giving back to the community.

Gotcha!

The ultimate paintball challenge!

UPEC is known for bringing paintball in the annual UPLB Feb Fair. Over the years, its activities were not only limited to paintball, but also Jousting or "UFC" (Ultimate Faluan Championship).

Suki

"Sumusulong at Umuunlad na Kabuhayan para sa Inyo"

SUKI is UPEC's community outreach program where it aims to teach livelihood programs to constituents of barangays near the UPLB campus. Seminars for hydroponics, vermicomposting, hair extensions crafting, and many others were taught in SUKI seminars over the years.
